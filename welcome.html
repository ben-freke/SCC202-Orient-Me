SCC.202 OrientMe! Experiment

Welcome to my SCC.202 OrientMe! Experiment. This experiment is designed to measure the time it takes to rotate two objects to the same orientation with two different methods, using the mouse and using the keyboard. This experiment has two interfaces, where will you will be given 6 rounds for each interface. This involves rotating the object 6 times with the mouse, then 6 times with the keyboard. During the experiment, the system will monitor the time and accuracy of your movements. This will not affect anything you see. Once you have finished the first experiment, you will automatically be sent to instructions to complete the next one.

Interface 1: Mouse & Buttons

You will be presented with buttons like the ones on the left. You must use these buttons to orientate the image. The buttons are in three categories, labled X, Y and Z. Each button increases or decreases the X, Y or Z value of the image depending on which button you click. You should aim to acheive the best possible match of the two images in the smallest possible time. To begin the experiment, click the Play button ( the top left button). Once you feel you have orientated the image as accurately as possible, click the Stop button (the top right button).
